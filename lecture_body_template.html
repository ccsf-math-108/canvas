Lectures

Lectures are a familiar way to passively learn new content. The passive component of lecturing means that it can be difficult to stay focused unless you are passionate about the particular lesson. Coding demonstrations will be included in the lecture along with a linked Jupyter notebook for you to follow along and stay engaged. Remember that this course requires practice, so practice with Python while you learn about the content!

  Instructions

  1. Attend the live lecture in-person or virtually. If you choose to attend virtually, Launch CityZoom and click the join link for the scheduled lecture in the Upcoming Meetings tab.
  2. Open the lecture materials linked below to follow along with the lecture and coding demonstrations.
  3. Once you are finished engaging with this lecture, select the "Mark as done " option on this page to track your progress.
  4. If the meting was recorded, view the recorded meeting in the Cloud Recordings tab.
  5. To help organize our continued conversations on the topics of this lecture, Reply to this page with any additional comments, questions, etc.
Launch CityZoom

  Lecture Materials

The weekly lectures typically contain various sub-lectures. You might want to follow along with the coding demonstrations or make notes on the Slides. The links to a template notebook will be shared before the lecture. After the lecture, a link will be provided to the complete version of the notebook from the meeting.
